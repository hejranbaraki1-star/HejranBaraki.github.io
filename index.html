About Me – My Journey

My name is Akbari Hejran, and I was born and raised in Kabul, the capital of Afghanistan. Kabul is a city of ancient history and vibrant culture, surrounded by the majestic Hindu Kush mountains. I grew up in a family that always prioritized education, even during challenging times. After finishing my primary education, I enrolled at Sayed Noor Mohammad Shah Mina in Kabul, where I completed my secondary education. Those years taught me perseverance, critical thinking, and the importance of dreaming big.

When I graduated from Sayed Noor Mohammad Shah Mina, I knew I wanted to explore the world beyond Afghanistan and gain knowledge that could help rebuild my homeland. I researched universities abroad and chose Weifang University in Shandong, China, because of its excellent Computer Science program and its welcoming environment for international students. In 2024, I started my journey as an international student, leaving my family and friends behind to pursue my dream.

My first semester at Weifang University was both challenging and exciting. I started with C Programming, which was my first real introduction to programming. C programming's clean syntax made it easy to understand core concepts like variables, loops, and functions. I spent many hours in the lab, solving algorithmic problems and building small applications. In the second semester, I moved to HTML, CSS, and JavaScript – the essential building blocks of the web. JavaScript, in particular, fascinated me because it brought interactivity to static pages. This portfolio website is the result of that learning: it combines structure (HTML), style (CSS), and dynamic behavior (JavaScript) to tell my story.

One of the things I love about computer science is that it is universal. No matter where you are from, code speaks the same language. I have collaborated with classmates from China, Pakistan, and Nigeria, and we have helped each other understand complex topics. I am also grateful to my professors at Weifang University, who have been supportive and encouraging. They not only teach the syllabus but also inspire us to be curious, ethical, and persistent.

Education & Learning Experience

My educational journey has been shaped by transitions – from Kabul to Weifang, from high school to university, and from being a beginner to becoming a confident programmer. At Sayed Noor Mohammad Shah Mina, I studied mathematics, physics, and English, which laid the foundation for my technical studies. But it was at Weifang University that I truly discovered my passion for computing.

In the first semester, the C Programming programming course was intense but rewarding. We covered variables, data types, loops, functions, lists, dictionaries, and file handling. I remember struggling with recursion at first – it felt abstract and confusing. But after writing dozens of small programs, I began to understand how to break down complex problems into smaller, manageable steps. That experience taught me that programming is not about memorizing syntax; it is about building a logical mindset. I also practiced on HackerRank and SoloLearn, solving problems and earning badges. These platforms gave me instant feedback and helped me improve step by step.

The second semester introduced web development. HTML and CSS gave me the power to structure and style content, but JavaScript was the real game-changer. I learned about DOM manipulation, event handling, and asynchronous programming. I built interactive forms, responsive layouts, and even a simple to-do list application. JavaScript showed me how dynamic and engaging the web can be. At the same time, I continued deepening my Python skills, using libraries like Matplotlib and Pandas for data visualization and analysis.

Beyond the classroom, I have been working on small projects to apply what I learn. For example, I built a personal diary application in Python that saves entries to a file, and I created a static portfolio website for a local business back in Kabul (as a practice project). These projects have given me confidence and shown me that programming can solve real problems. I also read articles and watch tutorials to stay updated, because technology changes quickly, and I want to keep learning even after graduation.

Looking ahead, I plan to study databases, backend development (Node.js or Django), and mobile app development in the coming semesters. My goal is to become a full-stack developer who can build complete web and mobile applications. I am also interested in artificial intelligence and how it can be used in education and healthcare – fields that are very relevant to Afghanistan. I believe that with hard work and the right skills, I can contribute to the development of my country and also build a meaningful career internationally.

This portfolio website is a milestone in my journey. It represents months of learning, trial and error, and the support of my teachers and friends. I have organized it into three pages: the homepage (which you are reading), a page for my web programming exercises (HTML, CSS, JavaScript), and a page for my Python programming work. Each page reflects a part of my learning and my identity as a student. I hope that whoever visits this site will see not only a collection of links but also the story of a determined student from Kabul who is building a future in computer science.
